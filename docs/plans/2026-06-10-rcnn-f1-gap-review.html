综合 agent 的完整归因论证(英文原文)

QUANTIFIED DECOMPOSITION OF THE "F1 GAP". Nominal gap: project numbers quoted as 0.75-0.81 vs literature 0.85-0.95, midpoint gap ~12pp. Three corrections must be applied before treating any of it as model deficit.

(1) The project's quoted band conflates three different metrics (eval auditor): the 0.800 unified_reviewall_A headline is PIXEL-level set-theoretic area F1 on 5 JHB val grids at a swept operating point, NOT polygon F1@0.5; the 0.709 CT independent_26 number is polygon PRESENCE F1 at IoU>=0.1 with pred-side many-to-one merge; Ch1 0.749 is human-review precision, not IoU-based. Honest polygon-level@IoU0.5 numbers: recall 68.8% (V3-C+SAM) to 79.6% (train20 per-det+SAM) on the JHB 25-grid clean_gt; strict-1:1 polygon F1 cross-domain 0.36 (vs 0.763 area F1 on the SAME predictions — i.e., the strict polygon number is dominated by GT carving, not detection failure).

(2) The literature's 0.85-0.95 is metrically incommensurable (both lit auditors agree): those are pixel-semantic F1/IoU on curated, PV-dense, in-domain chip datasets (USGS-CA 0.94, PV01 0.91, Heilbronn 0.96) or image-level classification (DeepSolar P93/R89). Essentially NO published rooftop-PV system reports instance/polygon F1@IoU0.5. The closest published object-level anchor is SolarMapper array-wise AP@IoU0.5 = 0.71-0.82 (Duke, clean conditions); the only published Mask R-CNN instance-level PV delineation F1 is ~0.73 (satellite)/0.68 (UAV). On true aerial orthophotos — ZAsolar's actual regime — published SOTA pixel F1 collapses to 0.74 (S3Former on IGN; all baselines IoU 0.39-0.59) and SolarDK's best pixel IoU is 0.72. ZAsolar's matched-metric pixel area_F1 of 0.80-0.85 in-domain is therefore AT OR ABOVE the aerial-orthophoto literature, and its polygon@0.5 performance sits inside the SolarMapper 0.71-0.82 object-AP band. Cross-domain, literature drops are WORSE than ZAsolar's (classification F1 0.98->0.46 cross-provider; DE->DK zero-shot 0.238; ZAsolar keeps bulk 1.04/R2 0.97 aggregate).

(3) GT noise/fragmentation caps measurable polygon F1 on the project's own suites: GT is 100% A2 SAM-drawn sub-array polygons (zero freehand installation-level gold anywhere), ~1.49 fragments per installation, matched-pair mean IoU only 0.73-0.74 (so boundary noise pushes true matches below the 0.5 IoU bar), clean_gt embeds accepted V3-C predictions (circularity), and literature quantifies GT-noise effects of this magnitude (SolarMapper audit moved precision 0.45->0.67; 42% of 'FPs' were correct; BDAPPV pre-consensus area correlation 0.61-0.68; humans miss ~3.8% of visible panels). Estimated measurable ceiling on current A2 GT: ~0.85-0.88 polygon F1@0.5 (merge profile) even for a near-perfect model.

ATTRIBUTION: of the ~12pp nominal gap, roughly 7-9pp (60-75%) is metric-type + benchmark-population non-comparability plus the GT-noise/fragmentation ceiling, and roughly 4-6pp (30-40%) is real, measured, addressable model deficit. The real deficit is concretely localized: (a) sub-array recall on multi-array roofs 0.30@IoU0.5 (Ch2 SAM_supp class), mid-size 30-100m2 recall 0.33, with G0925-class misses proven detector-bounded (proposal-stage, not postproc); (b) precision collapse confined to the s_xs x c_lo stratum (0.286 vs 0.78-1.00 elsewhere; 77% of small FPs are water heaters in the 5-20m2 band); (c) boundary halo (raw bulk 1.17) costing near-miss IoU conversions; (d) cross-domain per-grid dispersion (sigma_Bw 8x) from lookalike over-paint + dense-roof recall, with aggregate calibration intact.

REALISTIC TARGETS: in-domain polygon F1@0.5 under the installation (merge) profile against current A2 GT: 0.80-0.83 is achievable with the levers below; ~0.85-0.88 is the GT ceiling — chasing 0.90+ on current GT is chasing a metric artifact. Strict-1:1 polygon F1@0.5 in-domain: 0.72-0.78. On a new T1 installation-level gold suite (candidate C10), 0.85+ is plausible and would be the honest literature-comparable number alongside the pixel area_F1 0.80-0.85, which should be benchmarked against SolarMapper's 0.71-0.82 array AP and S3Former's 0.74 IGN-aerial pixel F1 — not against curated-chip 0.9+ numbers. The V1.4 demotion of per-polygon F1 to diagnostic in favor of aggregate calibration is exactly what the national-scale literature (DeepPVMapper, DeepSolar, SolarMapper recommendations) converged on.
